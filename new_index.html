Mass Shootings in the USA from 1966-2017


The Congressional Research Service defines mass shootings, as multiple, firearm, homicide incidents, involving 4 or more victims at one or more locations close to one another. Victims can also include shooters.
This data set covers over 300 records of mass shootings.


In total, there were 3430 Victims: 1435 Fatalities, 1995 Injured.




Locations of Mass Shootings and their Magnitudes

This map shows that states such as California, Texas, Florida and Illinois have a large number of mass shootings.

The largest mass shooting was in Las Vegas, Nevada in 2017.

Over 59 fatalities and 527 injuries were recorded in this event.




Heatmap of Annual Total Number of Victims

The heatmap shows that the month of February has the highest number of days where a mass shootings occured.

Mass shootings are also more likely to occur on the 7th day of a month.




Number of Mass Shootings by Cause

Overall, factors such as anger, terrorism, and mental issues are most often the cause of mass shootings.

In total, they account for 177 out of 323 total mass shooting events which is around 54% of the total




Total Number of Victims by Year

Overall, there is an increase in the danger level of mass shootings. After 2014, a sharp increase in the total number of victims can be observed.

While, 2017 was the year with the most number of victims in total with 558 injured and 113 dead, 2015 was the deadliest year with 200 injured and 226 dead.




Targets of Mass Shooters by Year

Over the years, there are a variety of targets that mass shooters have choosen.

2015 & 2016 have the largest variety of targets with 16 & 14 unique targets respectively
